Thermodynamics

The Thermodynamics,is the study of the relationship between heat (or energy) and work. In other words, thermodynamics looks at how we can put energy into a system and make it do work.

Types of Systems

Thermodynamic systems can be classified based on their interactions with the surroundings:

  • Open System: Can exchange both matter and energy with the surroundings.
  • Closed System: Can exchange energy but not matter with the surroundings.
  • Isolated System: Cannot exchange energy or matter with the surroundings.

Laws of thermodynamics

  • Zeroth Law: If two systems are in thermal equilibrium with a third system, they are in thermal equilibrium with each other.
  • First Law: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred or converted.
  • Second Law: The entropy of an isolated system not in equilibrium will tend to increase over time, approaching a maximum value at equilibrium.
  • Third Law: As a system approaches absolute zero, its entropy approaches a minimum value.

Important Terms In Thermodynamic

  • U = Change in internal energy of the system
  • Q = Heat added or removed from the system
  • W = Work done on or by the system
  • H = Enthalpy
  • S = Entropy
  • G = Gibbs free energy

Types of processes in thermodynamics

Isobaric Process

An isobaric process is a thermodynamic process in which the pressure remains constant. In such a process, the work done by or on the system is proportional to the change in volume.

Adiabatic Process

An adiabatic process is one where no heat is exchanged with the surroundings. The system is perfectly insulated, and changes in internal energy are due to work done on or by the system.

Isothermal Process

An isothermal process is a process in which the temperature remains constant. The heat added to the system is used to do work, without changing the internal energy of the system.

Isochoric Process

An isochoric process, or isovolumetric process, occurs at constant volume. Since there is no change in volume, no work is done, and any heat added increases the internal energy.

Intensive and Extensive properties

Intensive properties :An intensive property is a physical property of a system that does not depend on the system size or the amount of material in the system

Extensive properties :An extensive property is a property that depends on the amount of matter in a sample

Spontanity

The phrase spontaneity refers to a process viability. A spontaneous process is one that can occur on its own or as a result of some kind of initiation under certain conditions. In other terms, a spontaneous process is one that may happen without any intervention. Feasible or probable processes are other names for spontaneous processes.
